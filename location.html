Getting here

How to get to Abbazia di San Giusto

Flying in

The closest airport to fly into is Roma Fiumicino or Roma Ciampino. The next closest airports to fly into are Florence and Pisa. At the airport you can buy a local SIM card for tourists. It will be easier to get around and navigate Italy with the internet!

Getting to the venue

We suggest hiring a car as the venue is not easily accessible by public transportation. A car can be rented from Rome or Florence Airports or in each respective city center with lots of car rental options available. If your only option is public transportation, you can take a train from Roma Termini station to Viterbo City Center and then catch a 30 minute bus ride from Viterbo to Tuscania/

Places to visit

Suggestions on things to see and do around the Roma and Tuscania region

Spend a few days in Rome seeing the sights like the Pantheon, Colosseum, Spanish steps and Trevi Fountain. Depending on time, you should also spend at least half a day in the Vatican City where you will see famous frescos in the Sistine Chapel and St. Peter's Basillica.

Drive to Tuscania in time for the wedding festivities on October 20 to 21 before taking the scenic route up to Florence (a few hours north of the venue) the next day. Along the way stop at local vineyards for lunches and wine tastings (and potentially spend multiple days exploring the Tuscan countryside).

In Florence, a city of beautiful art, you will see Duomo, Uffizi Gallery, Piazzale Michelangelo in addition to Michelangelo's David before flying out of Florence.

If you have more time, you can carry on west to the Leaning Tower of Pisa and further north is the beautiful colourful coastal area of Cinque Terre. It all depends on how long you plan on being in Italy as there is a lot to see outside of just Rome, Florence and Tuscania (which is really a village).
